Still working on designing this website. 

The empty space under the content (most obviously on the landing page) seems too -- well, empty. I came across the concept of *finis*[^1]: a mark used by printers to signal the end of a book or other work. I like it.

I've currently settled on the leaf-like glyph `❧`: a typographical symbol often called a [*fleuron*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fleuron_(typography)). 

Top contenders were `❦`, which is another form of Aldine leaf[^2]; the Coptic Old Nubian Nyi, `ⲵ`, with its rigid, "grounded" architectural grid; and the Four Dot Punctuation, `⁘`, a glyph with a deep history in both ancient manuscripts and modern specialized notation. 

And to keep it all clean and [DRY](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don%27t_repeat_yourself), I implemented a tiny Astro component for it.

[^1]: 'From Middle English *finis*, from Latin *fīnis* ("end; limit").' --Wiktionary

[^2]: Named after the famous Renaissance printer, [Aldus Manutius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aldus_Manutius). Both are referred to as a *hedera* ("ivy leaf").
